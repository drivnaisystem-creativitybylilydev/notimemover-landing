## Brand Overview

- **Name:** NoTimeMover
- **Handle:** @notimemover (Instagram + Facebook — accounts being set up)
- **Tagline:** "No time to plan a move? We planned it for you."
- **Bio:** Boston's fast-response moving service. Book in 60 seconds. Most moves confirmed within 24 hours.
- **Service area:** Greater Boston — Allston, Brighton, Somerville, Jamaica Plain, South End, Back Bay, Charlestown, Cambridge + suburbs
- **Website:** notimemover.com
- **Positioning:** Speed-first local movers for last-minute, high-urgency moves. The antidote to slow, unreliable moving companies.
- **Mission:** Make booking a mover as fast and friction-free as possible — for people who don't have time to plan.
- **Hero service:** Local moves, Boston metro. Fast confirmation, transparent pricing, reliable crew.
- **Key differentiators:**
  - Book in 60 seconds online — no phone tag, no callback hell
  - Most moves confirmed within 24 hours
  - Built for last-minute situations — other movers don't want these jobs, we do
  - Speed-to-lead: real person calls back fast

## Target Audience

- **ICP:** Boston renters aged 22–38 who need to move fast — lease ending, other mover fell through, procrastinated, or just busy. The September 1 Allston/Brighton/Somerville turnover crowd is the core. Also busy professionals who value time over cost.
- **Secondary:** College students (Allston, Brighton, Somerville), real estate agents referring clients, property managers
- **Customer language:** "I need to move ASAP", "last minute", "this week", "reliable", "affordable", "can you fit me in", "my mover cancelled"
- **What they care about:** Speed of confirmation, reliability (will they actually show up), ease of booking, transparent pricing, not having to make 10 phone calls

**The coffee shimmer gradient** (used for headline accents only):
`#6B3A1F → #8B5230 → #C2784E → #8B5230 → #6B3A1F`

## Typography

- **Primary font:** Geist Sans — all UI, headings, captions, body
- **Editorial accent:** Instrument Serif (italic) — used very selectively for accent words or pull quotes
- **Mono:** Geist Mono — data, numbers, specs only
- **Heading weight:** 600, letter-spacing -0.025em
- **Case convention:** Sentence case on social. No ALL CAPS except eyebrow labels (10px, 0.22em tracking).
- **Text on visuals:** White (#FFFFFF) on dark backgrounds. Coffee accent (#6B3A1F) for highlighted words or numbers.
- **No decorative fonts.** Geist Sans handles everything — the brand's premium feel comes from spacing and weight, not font personality.

## Tone of Voice

- **Direct** — Say the thing. No warm-up sentences. No "Hey there!" openers. Lead with the point.
- **Urgent** — The brand name is the brand voice. Every caption should carry the feeling that time is a factor.
- **Confident** — Not aggressive, not salesy. We know what we do and we say it plainly.
- **Local** — Boston-specific references land harder than generic moving content. Mention actual neighbourhoods, Sept 1, the Allston Christmas, the realities of Boston leases.
- **Human** — Jermaine is the face of the business. Content that shows him and the crew doing real work outperforms anything generic. First person is fine.
- **Never:** inspirational fluff, "Exciting news!", "game-changer", "we're thrilled to announce", "in today's fast-paced world"

## Signature Content Format

NoTimeMover's most effective post pattern will be: **Real job → outcome → proof**.

```
[Real photo from the job — truck, crew, before/after, customer reaction]
     ↓
[Hook: specific situation or fact — "Booked Tuesday. Moved Thursday."]
     ↓
[Short story: what happened — 2-3 lines max]
     ↓
[CTA: book fast, DM us, link in bio]
```

This works because the brand promise is speed + reliability — the proof is showing it happened, not claiming it.

---

## Content Pillars

1. **Real jobs** — Photos/videos from actual moves. The most powerful content. Shows the crew, the truck, the job done.
2. **Speed proof** — "Booked [day], moved [day+2]." Short-turnaround testimonials, fast confirmation stories.
3. **Local Boston** — Sept 1 content, neighbourhood callouts, Allston/Brighton/Somerville specific. Speaks directly to the audience.
4. **How it works** — Booking flow, what to expect, "60 seconds to book" content. Reduces friction before they visit the site.
5. **Social proof** — Reviews, customer messages, completed job count milestones.
6. **Urgency / availability** — "We have openings this week." "Need to move before [date]?" Drives immediate action.

## Visual Photography Style

- **Dominant mood:** Dark, warm, premium. Not the beige/yellow brand most movers use.
- **Lighting:** Warm ambient, natural light when outdoors. Avoid flat overhead or harsh flash.
- **Composition:** Subject-forward — Jermaine or the crew are the anchor. Truck in frame when possible (brand asset).
- **Background treatment:** Real environments — apartments, streets, loading docks. Not staged studio shots.
- **Colour temperature:** Warm. Pull towards the coffee tones. Cool/blue tones don't fit.
- **For AI-generated visuals:** Dark background (`#050505`), warm accent lighting from coffee palette, urban Boston environment, Geist Sans for any text overlays.

## Do / Don't

### DO
- Show real jobs — photos of the crew doing actual moves are the highest-performing content type
- Use specific Boston neighbourhoods (Allston, Brighton, Somerville, Jamaica Plain, etc.)
- Reference Sept 1 and the Boston lease cycle — it's a cultural moment the audience knows
- Lead with speed: booking time, confirmation time, how fast the job was done
- Keep captions short and punchy — this is a service business, not a lifestyle brand
- One CTA per post. Either "Book at notimemover.com" or "DM us" — not both.

### DON'T
- Stock photography — generic moving truck photos, models who aren't Jermaine/the crew
- Emoji overload — 1-2 max, only when they add meaning
- Multiple hashtag styles (#moving #boston #movers #localmovers #affordablemovers #bostoncity…) — 3-6 targeted tags max
- Inspirational quotes about hard work or moving on in life — this is a utility service, not a wellness brand
- Post without a visual — every post needs an image or short video
- Pure white backgrounds — this brand lives in the dark palette
