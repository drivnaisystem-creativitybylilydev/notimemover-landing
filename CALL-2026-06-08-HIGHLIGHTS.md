## Key Discussion Points

### Recent Moving Jobs and Business Operations
NoTimeStorage discussed recent moving jobs, including a significant move from Massachusetts to New Jersey found via Facebook Marketplace. The job had a 50-60% profit margin, with a four-hour drive each way and some logistical delays due to a late team member. NoTimeStorage has completed two moves so far (one on Monday, one on Saturday) and currently has no jobs lined up. The business is in its early stages, with NoTimeStorage going all-in and able to handle one move per day. The team consists of former teammates on a rotation, with plans to hire more as demand increases. Van rentals are handled through U-Haul, with costs for mileage and insurance factored into pricing.

### Lead Generation and Marketing Challenges
Lead sourcing is primarily through Facebook Marketplace and groups, but all four Facebook posts made by NoTimeStorage were taken down, likely due to algorithm detection. Some posts generated interest, but the flexible pricing model (letting customers set the price) confused potential clients, leading to lost jobs. NoTimeStorage has not found Craigslist or Nextdoor effective due to saturation and fees. Response time to leads is immediate, but the main challenge is overcoming skepticism and building trust. The team is considering automating outreach and follow-ups, and Finn proposes using tools like demand scrapers and automated SMS to improve lead conversion and reduce ghosting.

### Pricing Model and Customer Experience
NoTimeStorage uses a flexible pricing model, asking customers for their budget and then quoting slightly below to win jobs. This approach is unique but has caused confusion and skepticism among potential clients, who are not used to setting their own price for moving services. The quoting process is being refined to include a website form where customers can select move type, location, and budget, with default and recommended prices to guide them. Payment methods have included Venmo and cash, with payment typically at the end of the move or split between on-load and offload for larger jobs. Finn and NoTimeStorage discussed improving the quoting form to better influence customer expectations and reduce confusion.

### Business Development, Automation, and Technology Plans
Finn outlined a plan to build out the business's digital infrastructure, including a CRM, Google Sheets dashboard, automated SMS for lead response, and a fully optimized website with SEO and Google Business Profile integration. The goal is to automate as much of the lead management and quoting process as possible, including follow-ups and spam prevention. Finn also discussed using demand scrapers to monitor platforms like Facebook, Craigslist, and Reddit for moving leads, and potentially implementing an AI receptionist for phone inquiries. The initial focus is on sealing operational leaks before scaling up lead generation and ad spend. Implementation is expected to take 7-16 days once agreed upon.

### Equity, Milestones, and Partnership Terms
Finn and NoTimeStorage discussed an equity arrangement starting at 35%, with escalation to 45% based on milestones. Proposed milestones include completing 10 consistent jobs, achieving a $3,000 month, and completing a job without NoTimeStorage's direct involvement (full outsourcing). NoTimeStorage expressed concern that some milestones (like $3,000 in 10 jobs) may not reflect true consistency and prefers milestones that show sustained growth, such as achieving a move per day. Both parties agree on the importance of aligning equity increases with meaningful business progress and are open to further discussion and proposal refinement.

### Financials, Costs, and Marketing Spend
The team discussed how to handle costs related to digital infrastructure, marketing, and ad spend. NoTimeStorage asked about Finn's financial responsibilities and costs for maintaining the digital side, including lead sourcing and marketing. Finn suggested that ad spend strategy would determine cost-sharing, proposing a possible $10/day ad budget for 30 days as an example. Both agreed to revisit the financial arrangement based on the chosen marketing strategy. The current website retainer is $49/month, which will continue alongside the equity deal. Finn will also handle SEO, Google Business Profile, and blog content to improve organic reach before investing heavily in ads.

### Legal, Licensing, and Trust Considerations
Finn asked about the accuracy of the claim on the website that NoTimeStorage is licensed and insured. NoTimeStorage clarified that they are insured but not yet licensed, as it doesn't make sense to obtain a license before having sufficient jobs. Finn emphasized the importance of not misrepresenting licensing status on the website to avoid legal issues and build trust with customers. Both agreed to update the website to accurately reflect the current status. Trust is also a key concern in customer interactions, especially given the skepticism around the flexible pricing model and the vulnerability customers feel when hiring movers.
