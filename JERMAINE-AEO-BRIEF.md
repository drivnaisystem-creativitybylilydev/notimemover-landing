## Why This Matters (Non-Technical Version)

When someone in Boston types "movers near me" into Google, into ChatGPT, into Siri, or into Perplexity — each of those platforms pulls business recommendations from different sources. Google uses your Google Business Profile. ChatGPT uses Yelp and BBB. Siri uses Apple Maps. Copilot uses Bing.

Right now NoTimeMover is invisible on most of these because the profiles aren't set up yet.

The companies showing up — Safe Responsible Movers, Gentle Giant, etc. — have been on these platforms for years with hundreds of reviews. We can't close that gap in a week. But we can start the process now, during moving season, and compound it through the summer.

**The 1.2% stat:** A 2026 study of 350,000 local businesses found that ChatGPT only recommends 1.2% of them. Getting into that pool isn't about luck — it's about being present on the specific platforms AI systems trust. This doc is the roadmap to get there.

## Platform-by-Platform Breakdown

### 1. Google Business Profile (GBP)
**Powers:** Google Search, Google Maps, Gemini AI, Google AI Overviews

This is the highest-leverage platform — Gemini has 100% accurate data from GBP and will recommend businesses directly from it.

**What Finn handles:**
- Writing the full profile description, services, service areas
- Setting categories, hours, website URL
- Adding FAQ content to the website (feeds Google's AI answers)
- Setting up the review request link to send customers

**What Finn needs from Jermaine:**
- [ ] **Business phone number** — needs to be real and consistent everywhere. What number do customers currently use to reach you?
- [ ] **Video verification** — Google requires the owner to record a short video showing the business operating (truck, equipment, crew) to verify the listing. Finn can't do this remotely. Jermaine does it from his phone through the GBP app.
- [ ] **Photos** — minimum 5, ideally 10+. Crew in action, truck, boxes being moved, team shot. Listings with 10+ photos get 35% more clicks. These need to be real photos from real jobs.
- [ ] **Confirm coverage area** — are you willing to take jobs in Worcester, Springfield, Lowell? Or strictly Greater Boston for now?

**What Finn handles:**

### 3. BBB (Better Business Bureau)
**Powers:** ChatGPT specifically — BBB leads ChatGPT with 68,000+ citations per quarter, more than any other platform on that AI

BBB A+ accreditation is the single most cited trust signal on ChatGPT for home services businesses. It's a 6-8 week application process.

**What Finn handles:**
- Submitting the accreditation application, writing the business description

**What Finn needs from Jermaine:**
- [ ] **Legal business name** (registered entity name — LLC, sole prop, etc.)
- [ ] **Business address** — BBB requires a verifiable address even for service area businesses. Can use a PO Box or a registered agent address if Jermaine doesn't want to list a home address.
- [ ] **Certificate of insurance** — BBB verifies that moving companies are actually insured. Jermaine needs to share a copy of the insurance certificate.
- [ ] **Business license or registration** — whatever Jermaine has that shows the business is legally operating.
- [ ] **Year founded** — what year did you start doing moves under NoTimeMover?
