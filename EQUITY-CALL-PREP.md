## Questions to Ask

### His commitment
- Is this full-time or does he have another job right now?
- How much of his own money has he put in so far?
- What does his week actually look like — how many hours on this?
- If he gets a job offer or moves city, what's the exit plan?

### Fulfillment reality
- Owns a truck or renting per job? What does truck rental cost?
- How many jobs can he do per week realistically, alone?
- Does he have a second mover lined up or is every job solo?
- Does he have actual liability insurance in place — not "planning to"?
- Is he MA-registered as a for-hire mover? (State licensing requirements exist — if not compliant, the business is legally exposed)

### The marketplace vs reality
- Phase 1: him doing every move himself, or already talking to other movers?
- When does he realistically see himself off the truck into a coordinator role?
- What's his actual 90-day target — number of jobs, revenue?

### What he expects from you
- How many hours per week does he think this requires from you?
- Does he expect same-day availability for site changes or issues?
- Who has final say on pricing, marketing copy, offers?
- If he wants to pivot the product or brand, does he need your sign-off?
- Does he expect you on customer calls or is that fully his lane?

### Money and costs
- Any cash component at all, even small — or pure equity?
- Who pays for Google Ads when you run them? **(Must be him)**
- Who owns and pays for Google Cloud account (Distance Matrix API)?
- Who owns the Vercel account and domain — who pays those bills?
- If the business closes, what happens to the code and systems you built?

His answer immediately tells you if he understands fulfillment is 100% his responsibility, or if he'll blame the digital side when jobs don't close.

## Full Call Frame

### OPEN — 2 min

> "Hey Jermaine, good to connect. I want to make this call useful for both of us — I've got some questions for you, you probably have some for me, and by the end I want us to walk away with a clear picture of whether this makes sense and what it looks like if it does. Sound good?"

---

### HIS VISION — 5 min, you listen

> "Before I get into anything on my end — where's your head at with NoTimeMover right now? Like where do you see this in 6 months, realistically?"

Listen for: is this full-time or a side project, real marketplace vision or still "me in a truck", financial commitment, whether his timeline matches yours.

Follow-up: *"And right now, are you doing every move yourself or do you already have other movers lined up?"*

---

### FULFILLMENT REALITY — 5 min

> "I want to make sure I understand the operation side because what I build needs to match what you can actually fulfill."

- Truck situation — own or rent per job?
- Solo or do you have a second mover?
- Realistically, how many jobs per week can you handle right now?
- Insurance and MA licensing — is that in place?

> "I ask because the scraper I want to build can surface leads fast — like within minutes of someone posting online. If I'm sending you 10 leads a week, I need to know you can close and fulfill them."

---

### WHAT HE EXPECTS FROM YOU — 5 min

> "I want to set expectations clearly on my side too. What does working with me look like in your head — how many hours a week do you think this takes from me?"

Then: > "Here's how I see my lane: I own everything digital. The site, SEO, the scraper, the CRM, the automations. I'm not on customer calls, I'm not doing same-day emergency fixes at midnight, and I'm not funding ad spend — that comes from the business. My job is to make sure leads are coming in and nothing falls through the cracks. Does that match what you had in mind?"

---

### THE AUTOMATION VISION — 5 min, this is your sell

> "Let me tell you what I'm actually planning to build, because I think this is the part that separates NoTimeMover from every other guy with a truck in Boston."

- 50+ neighborhood SEO pages — people searching "movers in Allston" find you organically
- GBP fully built so you show up on Maps
- The scraper — 24/7 monitoring Reddit, Craigslist, Facebook Groups. Someone posts "need a mover this weekend in Somerville" — you get a text in minutes with a pre-written message ready to send. You tap, it goes. Before any competitor even sees the post.
- CRM so no lead ever falls through — every one tracked, status visible, follow-ups automated
- After every job, auto-text goes out asking for a Google review

> "No moving company at your level does this. They wait for the phone to ring. We go get the jobs."

Then ask: *"How are you finding jobs right now — is it mostly inbound, or are you doing any outreach?"*

---

### MONEY & TERMS — 5 min

> "Alright, let's talk terms. Here's where I'm at."

- 35% now, written escalation to 45% at three milestones: first 10 consistent jobs, first delegated mover, first $3k month
- Retainer stays at $49/month — keeps the site live, separate from equity work
- Ad spend comes from the business, not from me — I manage, you fund
- Something in writing before I start — Google Doc minimum
- Starting in ~2 months — already communicated, holding that

> "The reason I'm firm on the milestones in writing is clarity protects both of us."

---

### THE ONE QUESTION

> "One thing I want to be direct about — if I bring in 20 leads in the first month and you can only fulfill 4 of them, whose problem is that?"

Let him answer. Don't fill the silence.

---

### CLOSE — 3 min

> "Here's what I think makes sense as next steps. I'll put together a simple one-pager — terms, milestones, what I'm building, what you're responsible for. You look at it, we either agree or we adjust one thing at a time. Does that work?"

If he pushes back on terms: *"What specifically doesn't work for you? I'm open to adjusting one thing at a time, but the milestones stay specific — that's non-negotiable."*

---

### IF IT GOES SIDEWAYS

If he comes back with 25% again:
> "I walked back from 50 to 35. That was already a concession. At 25% I'm doing agency-level work for less than a junior partner stake. I can't do that."

If he wants to "revisit after 90 days" with no milestones:
> "90 days with no defined target isn't a timeline, it's a delay. Give me the numbers and I'll hold you to them."
